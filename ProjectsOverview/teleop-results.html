Quantitative & Qualitative Results

Teleoperation – Results

Impact of auditory, audio-to-visual, and combined feedback on response time, errors, and situational awareness during emergency takeover.

Primary Statistical Outcome

No statistically significant differences observed

One-way ANOVA revealed no significant effect of feedback modality on response time (F = 0.27, p = .76). However, consistent performance trends emerged across conditions.

Audio Only

Fastest trend

Participants responded more quickly when relying solely on auditory cues, without competing visual information.

Audio → Visual

Comparable trend

Converting audio cues into visual alerts showed similar response patterns, without measurable performance gains.

Audio + Visual

Slower trend

Simultaneous multimodal cues increased cognitive demand, often leading to delayed reactions.

Qualitative Insights (Post-study Interviews)

  • • “Audio alone was enough to understand what was happening.”
  • • “With both audio and visuals, I sometimes didn’t know where to focus.”
  • • Participants reported information overload when multiple cues appeared simultaneously.
lightbulb

More information is not always better: audio-only cues can support faster, clearer decisions in time-critical teleoperation.
