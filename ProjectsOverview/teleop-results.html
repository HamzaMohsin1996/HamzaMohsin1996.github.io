Key Finding

Modality count does not equal performance.

Sensory over-saturation increases cognitive load, resulting in significantly slower reaction times for multi-modal stimuli compared to isolated auditory cues.

Contrary to common assumptions in interface design, increasing sensory modalities does not always yield superior results. In this HCI trial, the Audio-only condition provided a clearer, faster signal for human operators. This suggests that for high-speed teleoperation tasks, visual feedback may serve as a distractor rather than a complement, particularly when reaction thresholds are below 300ms.
