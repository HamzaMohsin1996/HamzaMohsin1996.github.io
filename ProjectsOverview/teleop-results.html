Quantitative & Qualitative Results

Teleoperation – Results

Impact of auditory, audio-to-visual, and combined feedback on response time, errors, and situational awareness during emergency takeover.

Primary Statistical Outcome

No statistically significant differences observed

One-way ANOVA revealed no significant effect of feedback modality on response time (F = 0.27, p = .76). However, consistent performance trends emerged across conditions.

Final Interface Designs Evaluated in Study

Audio Only

Participants relied solely on auditory cues without competing visual information.

Audio → Visual

Auditory events were translated into symbolic on-screen alerts.

Audio Only

Fastest trend

Clear auditory cues supported rapid responses with minimal cognitive overhead.

Audio → Visual

Comparable trend

Visual translation neither improved nor degraded performance significantly.

Audio + Visual

Slower trend

Concurrent cues increased cognitive demand and delayed reactions.

Qualitative Insights (Post-study Interviews)

  • • “Audio alone was enough to understand what was happening.”
  • • “With both audio and visuals, I didn’t know where to focus.”
  • • Participants reported information overload with multimodal feedback.
lightbulb

More information is not always better: audio-only feedback supported the clearest and fastest decisions.
