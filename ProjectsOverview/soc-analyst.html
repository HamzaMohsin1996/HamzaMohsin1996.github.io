Problem Context

SOC Alert Triage

Security Operations Center analysts make early incident response decisions by interpreting alerts, logs, and system context under time pressure and accountability constraints.

Incident response requires analysts to construct and revise interpretations from incomplete and heterogeneous information.

Early triage decisions shape downstream technical and organizational outcomes.

References: Lakshmi et al. 2021
Core Challenge

Why Triage Is Hard

Analysts must integrate alerts, logs, and telemetry to form incident interpretations during early-stage triage.

Existing SOC tools fragment information across views, increasing cognitive effort and reducing situational awareness.

Information needs evolve over time, but representations remain static.

References: De Valk & Elmqvist 2024
AI in Practice

What AI Supports Today

Current SOC platforms apply machine learning primarily to improve detection throughput, while interpretation and responsibility remain human.

Alert prioritization
Event correlation
Investigation summaries

Analysts are positioned mainly as evaluators of AI output, not collaborators in constructing understanding.

References: NIST SP 800-61 Jiang et al. 2022 Radiant Security 2025
Research Gap

What Is Missing

  • No models of analyst–AI interaction.
  • Unclear decision boundaries and responsibility shifts.
  • Limited interaction design for collaboration.
  • Missing accountability over time.
  • No empirical evaluation of co-constructive interfaces.
References: Bansal et al. 2024 Carroll et al. 2024
