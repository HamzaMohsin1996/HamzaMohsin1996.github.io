Master’s Thesis · Context

UAVs in Emergency Dispatch

Emergency dispatchers coordinate incidents under high workload, frequent interruptions, and time pressure, requiring rapid re-engagement with evolving incident information.

warning Core Problem

UAVs provide early aerial information, but existing UAV interfaces are designed for field responders, not for dispatchers who must re-engage with incidents after interruptions.

Master’s Thesis · Study Design

Experimental Design

Design

  • • Within-subjects design
  • • Counterbalanced conditions
  • • N = 29 participants

Task

Simulated emergency dispatching task involving multitasking, enforced interruptions, and later re-engagement with ongoing incidents.

Independent Variable

Interface condition: with vs without structured UAV information support.

Master’s Thesis · Measures

Outcome Measures & Instrumentation

Primary Measures

  • • Situational Awareness (SPAM)
    Probe accuracy and response time during task execution
  • • Re-engagement Speed
    Time to correctly respond after enforced interruptions

Secondary Measures

  • • Cognitive workload (DRT)
  • • Subjective situational awareness (SART)
  • • Usability (SUS)

hub Measurement Infrastructure

  • • Unity simulation
    Logged UAV state, incident events, and probe timing
  • • Web-based dispatcher interface
    Logged interactions, responses, and navigation behavior
  • • Synchronized logging
    Timestamped events linked via trial and participant IDs

This enabled precise alignment of system events and user responses across the simulation and frontend.

Master’s Thesis · Findings

Key Findings & Insight

check_circle Key Result

Structured UAV information significantly improved re-engagement accuracy and response time after interruptions, without increasing workload or task time.

insights Design Insight

Continuous video alone is insufficient. Interaction design determines whether information supports or hinders dispatcher sensemaking during re-engagement.

This insight directly motivated my PhD proposal on human–AI collaboration in high-stakes decision making.
Proposal · Research Gap

Why Current AI Support Falls Short

SOC alert triage is a sensemaking process in which analysts construct and revise incident interpretations under time pressure.

Existing AI systems optimize throughput and classification, but do not support how analysts reason, negotiate decisions, or retain responsibility.

As a result, there is no empirical model of analyst–AI interaction, decision boundaries, or accountability during triage.

warning

Gap: AI is evaluated as output quality, not as a collaborative decision partner.

Key references: Lakshmi et al. 2021 De Valk & Elmqvist 2024 Bansal et al. 2024

Proposal · Methodology

Research Design

The methodology follows a structured HCI pipeline in which each phase produces artifacts that directly constrain the next.

visibility

Phase 1 · Analysis

Empirical study of analyst–AI interaction through observation, task analysis, and interviews.

Output: workflow and decision-boundary model

architecture

Phase 2 · Design

Design of co-constructive interaction mechanisms that allow analyst feedback and influence.

Output: interactive triage interface

analytics

Phase 3 · Evaluation

Controlled studies comparing baseline AI support with co-construction.

Output: empirical evidence across conditions
