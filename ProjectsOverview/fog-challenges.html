Design Reflection

Fog Interface — Challenges & Decisions

Challenge

Fog removes reliable depth perception. Visual clutter or over-precise graphics risk distracting drivers in already stressful conditions.

Design Decision

Use abstract distance bars and a vehicle-centric icon instead of numeric distances to reduce cognitive load.

Evaluation Insight

Non-significant statistics highlighted limitations of sample size and lab realism, not necessarily interface usefulness.

Lessons Learned

  • Interface design can be valuable even without statistical significance.
  • Fog requires abstraction, not precision.
  • Future studies need real driving contexts and larger samples.
